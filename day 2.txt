1)Understand the difference between block-level and inline elements

 Practice examples with <div>, <p> (block) and <span>, <a> (inline)?

Block-level elements like <div> or <p> start on a new line and take the full width of the page. They are used for bigger sections of content.

Inline elements like <span> or <a> stay in the same line and only take as much space as their content. They are used for styling small parts of text.

example 

<!DOCTYPE html>
<html>
<head>
    <title>My College</title>
</head>
<body>

    <div style="border: solid; background-color: lightblue;">
        <h1>SKP Engineering College</h1>
    </div>

    <p style="border: solid; background-color: lightgreen;">
        Welcome to our college website. We offer many courses for students.
    </p>

    <p>
        Our college provides quality education in 
        <span style="border: solid; background-color: yellow;">Engineering and Technology</span>.
        Visit the 
        <a href="https://www.skpengg.ac.in/" style="border: solid; background-color: orange;">College Website</a>
        for more details.
    </p>

</body>
</html>

In this example:

<div> and <p> are block elements

<span> and <a> are inline elements
