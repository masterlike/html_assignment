1)Learn what semantic tags are and why they are important for SEO & accessibility. Compare semantic vs non-semantic tags.

In HTML, semantic tags are elements that describe the purpose of the content inside them. They help developers, browsers, and search engines understand the structure of a webpage.

For example:

<header> - represents the top part of the webpage .

<main> → represents the main content of the page.

<section> - represents a group of related content.

<article> - represents independent content like a blog or news.

<aside> - represents side content like ads or extra information.

<footer> - represents the bottom part of the page 

SEO (Search Engine Optimization)
Semantic tags help search engines understand the structure and content of the webpage, which improves website ranking.

Accessibility
Semantic tags help screen readers and assistive technologies understand the webpage structure, making it easier for people with disabilities to navigate the site

difference between semantic vs non semantic tags

Semantic tags is give meaning to content and Improve SEO and accessibility
Example: <header>, <footer>, <section>


non semantic tags are do not describe the content and Used mainly for styling or grouping
Example: <div>, <span>
